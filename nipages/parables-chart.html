This chart catalogues the parables of Jesus as distinct narrative illustrations or extended metaphors. Brief similes ("You are the salt of the earth") and figurative statements are not included. Scholars count differently depending on classification; this reference follows a broadly accepted enumeration in Catholic scholarship. Luke's Gospel contains the most parables — and the most unique ones — reflecting Luke's particular emphasis on God's mercy toward the lost, the poor, and the marginalized.

Catholic interpretive notes draw on patristic sources, the Catechism of the Catholic Church, and the theological tradition. The parables are not mere moral lessons — they are revelations of the Kingdom, disclosing the inner logic of God's saving action in Christ and His Church.
