Thai Tones

Thai has 5 tones. The same syllable with a different tone means something completely different.

กา
Mid Tone (สามัญ)
Flat, neutral pitch — like stating a fact calmly
ก่า
Low Tone (เอก)
Pitch drops low and stays — like a disappointed "oh"
ก้า
Falling Tone (โท)
Starts high, drops sharply — like an emphatic "No!"
ก๊า
High Tone (ตรี)
Pitch rises high — like an excited question
ก๋า
Rising Tone (จัตวา)
Dips low then rises — like a surprised "Really?"

Survival Tips

🙏 ครับ / ค่ะ — Polite Particles

As a male, add ครับ (kráp) to end of sentences. Your wife uses ค่ะ (kâ).

😊 Smile — It Goes Far

Thailand is the "Land of Smiles." Even rough Thai + a smile goes a very long way.

🌶️ Ordering Food

ไม่เผ็ด (mâi pèt) = not spicy. เผ็ดนิดหน่อย (pèt nít nòi) = a little spicy.

🗣️ No Verb Conjugation!

Thai verbs never change. "Go" is always ไป (bpai) no matter what.

🔢 555 = LOL

5 = ห้า (hâa). So 555 = "hahaha." Thai texting shorthand!

🙏 The Wai

Palms together at chest with slight bow. Higher hands = more respect. Don't wai children or service staff first.
